APRIL 2016


The first official NASA Swarmathon competition began at Kennedy's Swamp Works facility in Florida. Each of the twelve competing teams developed search algorithms which were uploaded to the small robots nicknamed, "Swarmies" to get them to perform an autonomous search for resources on Mars.

SEPTEMBER 2016

University of New Mexico shipped the robotic kits to Montgomery College along with safety documents. The grippers and April cubes were shipped later. Shortly after, UNM provide a webinar on the proper way to assemble the robot kits.

OCTOBER 2016

UNM hosted another webinar introducing the Virtual and Physical teams to the competition. Finalized rules were sent to each team. A compilation of tips, tricks, and hacks were sent to the physical teams.

JANUARY 2017

A student orientation was held virtually for both physical and virtual teams.

FEBRUARY 2017

First code check-in was submitted along with student survery submittals. NASA hosted a Virtual Opportunity Fair. The Distributed Research Experience for Undergraduates (DREU) Application was submitted. A second code check-in was submitted. Faculty survey was due for submittal as well as technical question and another code upload.

MARCH 2017

Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) Application was submitted. Montgomery College submitted their Team Story Video. Another code check-in occurred. A student seurvey was due. REU and DREU Recommendation Letters from faculty mentors for their student will be requested by email and due. MC registered for NASA KSC Passes along with the file code check-in. The Outreach Report was submitted.

APRIL 2017

All technical reports were submitted. NASA kicked off the Swarmathon competition. MC took first place in the Virtual Competition.
